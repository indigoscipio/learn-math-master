8.3.11
suppose 0 < p < 100
if we increase 100 by p%
then decrease the new quantity by p%
what is the final quantity in terms of p? is it larger or smaller than 100?

answer: 
original amount = 100

increase
p+100/100 . 100
(1 + p/100) . 100 = 100 + p

decrease
(100 + p) . 100-p/100
(100 + p) . (1 - p/100)
(100 . (1 - p/100)) + (p . (1 - p/100))

100 . (1 - p/100)
100 . -100p/100
100 - p


 p . (1 - p/100)
 p - p^2/100

(100 - p) + (p - p^2/100)
100 - p^2/100

b. suppose 0 < q < 100
if we decrease 100 by q% and increase the new quantity by q%
then what is the final quantity in terms of q
is it larger or smaller than 100?

decrease 100 by q%
100-q/100 . 100
(1 - q/100) . 100
100 - q


(100-q) . (1 + q/100)
100 . (1 + q/100) + -q . (1 + q/100)
100 - q^2/100

/*
PERCENTAGE ASYMETRY
it doesn't matter, the -% will always overpower
When you increase first, the decrease acts on a larger number (the "peak").
When you decrease first, the increase has to work harder because it's starting from a smaller base (the "valley").
*/
